Course Info

Weekly Plan

Each week, there will be one prerecorded lecture posted to Canvas, as well as a Jupyter notebook walking through important examples. On Thursdays at 1:30 pm EST, we will meet via Zoom for a discussion section. The purpose of this discussion is to deepen your understanding of the course context, highlight helpful case studies, and answer questions about the material. The weekly discussions are not mandatory, and will be recorded and posted to Canvas afterward.

Questions and Clarifications

Rather than email the teaching staff directly, we ask that you post questions about the course to Piazza. If your question is sensitive or private in nature, our email addresses are listed on Canvas.

Homework

The course has two due dates for homework assignments: February 28th and April 12th, both at 11:59 pm EST. Homework 1 covers the historical vision unit, and Homework 2 covers the learned vision unit. Both homeworks have a small number of required problems, as well as a handful of flexible problems. You must complete at 3 of the flexible problems to receive credit for the homework. If you complete more than three, we will choose three to grade and the rest will count towards your recovered credit.

Recovering Credit

If you are unsatisfied with your score on one of the two homework sets, you can regain some credit by completing one or more of the remaining flexible problems. You regain one-third of your score on these problems, for a maximum of 100% on the homework set. These can be turned in any time before the end of the semester.

Late Policy

The purpose of structuring the homework like this is to 1) allow you to play to your strengths, as vision is a field of diversely skilled researchers; 2) provide more opportunities to connect material from several lectures into a single setting; and 3) grant flexibility with the deadlines. If you know you will need to submit an assignment late, email Parker as early as possible to make arrangements for an adjusted due date. In the absence of an agreement that your assignment will be late, you will lose 10% per day it is late.

Course Project

In lieu of a final exam, you will conduct a course project in teams of 2-4. Details about the project, including several suggested topics, will be released later in the semester. After the submission deadline for Homework 2, the weekly Zoom discussions will stop, and additional office hours will take their place, for the explicit purpose of project help. Similarly, the lectures after Homework 2 cover supplemental topics, and are optional.

Grading

Your final score in the course is a weighted sum of your scores on the two homework sets and the final project. Among the three scores, your highest score is worth 40% of your final score. The second-highest is worth 35% and the lowest is 25%. Barring a dramatic distribution of these scores, your final letter grade will be as follows (round to the nearest 1%):

Academic Honesty

While you are (highly!) encouraged to discuss homework assignments with other students, your programming work must be completed individually. You may not search for solutions online, or use existing implementations of the algorithms in the assignments. Please see the Michigan engineering honor code for more information.
